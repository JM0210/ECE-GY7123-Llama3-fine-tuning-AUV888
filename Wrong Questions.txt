#### QUESTION 100 ####
Triangle $ABC$ has side lengths $AB=5$, $BC=6$, and $AC=7$. Two bugs start simultaneously from $A$ and crawl along the perimeter of the triangle in opposite directions at the same speed. They meet at point $D$. What is $BD$?

#### SOLUTION ####
Let $AB = c, BC = a, CA = b$ and $P$ be the perimeter of the triangle $ABC$.
Let $L$ be the distance between $A$ and $D$ and $L'$ the distance between $D$ and $B$.
Since the bugs crawl along the perimeter of the triangle in opposite directions at the same speed, $L = L'$.
Therefore, the total distance that each bug crawls is $L + L' = L + L = P$, i.e., $L + L = P$.
So $M + BD = P \Rightarrow BD = P - M = \boxed{N}$.

#### REFERENCE ANSWER ####
N

#### QUESTION 120 ####
If I choose four cards from a standard $52$-card deck, with replacement, what is the probability that I will end up with one card from each suit?

#### SOLUTION ####
Let $X$ be the number of cards from each suit that we obtain after drawing 4 cards with replacement.
Then $X$ is a Multinomial random variable with $n=4$ and $p_1=p_2=p_3=p_4=1/4$.
Then the probability of obtaining one card from each suit is \begin{align*}
P(X_1=1, X_2=1, X_3=1, X_4=1) &= \frac{4!}{1! 1! 1! 1!} \left( \frac{1}{4} \right)^4\\
&= \frac{1}{4^4} = \boxed{\frac{1}{256}}
\end{align*}

#### REFERENCE ANSWER ####
\frac{1}{256}

#### QUESTION 160 ####
How many paths are there from $A$ to $B$, if every step must be up or to the right?[asy]size(4cm,4cm);int w=6;int h=5;int i;pen p=fontsize(9);for (i=0; i<h; ++i){draw((0,i) -- (w-1,i));}for (i=0; i<w; ++i){draw((i, 0)--(i,h-1));}label("$A$", (0,0), SW, p);label("$B$", (w-1,h-1), NE, p);[/asy]

#### SOLUTION ####
We can view the given diagram as an $n\times n$ lattice where the number of lattice points is $N=n^2$.
How many paths are there from $(0,0)$ to $(N,N)$ which uses steps of $(1,1)$ or $(1,-1)$?
We can define this problem as a recursive definition:
\[a(i,j) = a(i-1,j) + a(i,j-1)\]
where $a(i,j)$ represents the number of paths from $(0,0)$ to $(i,j)$.
We also have $a(i,0) = a(0,j) = 1$ since we start at $(0,0)$ and there is only 1 way to get from $(0,0)$ to any of the points in the right or top row since the only steps available to us are $(1,1)$ or $(1,-1)$.
Let's compute these numbers using dynamic programming.
<llm-code>
n = 6
matrix = [[0]*n for i in range(n)]
for i in range(n):
    matrix[0][i] = 1
for j in range(n):
    matrix[j][0] = 1

for i in range(1, n):
    for j in range(1, n):
        matrix[i][j] = matrix[i-1][j] + matrix[i][j-1]
print(matrix[n-1][n-1])
</llm-code>
<llm-code-output>
252
</llm-code-output>
So there are $\boxed{252}$ such paths.

\[\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 7 & -3 \end{pmatrix}\]to $S,$ we obtain the region $S'.$  Find the area of $S'.$

#### SOLUTION ####
The question is about applying a linear transformation on the region $S$.
This transformation is determined by the matrix \[\begin{pmatrix}2 & 1 \\ 7 & -3\end{pmatrix}.\]
The area of the transformed region $S'$ is then the determinant of the above matrix multiplied by the area of $S$.
<llm-code>
import sympy as sp

# matrix A
A = [[2, 1], [7, -3]]

# determinant of A
det_A = sp.Matrix(A).det()

# area of S
area_S = 10

# area of S'
area_S_prime = abs(det_A) * area_S
print(area_S_prime)
</llm-code>

130
</llm-code-output>
So the area of $S'$ is \boxed{130}.
